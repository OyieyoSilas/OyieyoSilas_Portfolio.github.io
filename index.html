Projects' Summary.

Agriculture & Sustainability

In the field of agriculture, sustainability and environment, data analysis plays a crucial role in monitoring climate change, resource usage, and ecological impacts. One of my projects involved analyzing global CO2 emissions and deforestation datasets using satellite imagery and environmental monitoring data. By applying time-series forecasting and geospatial analysis, the project identified key trends in carbon footprints across regions, predicted future deforestation risks, and provided actionable insights for policy recommendations to promote renewable energy adoption and reduce environmental degradation.

Economics and Development

Economics and development rely heavily on data to understand growth patterns, poverty reduction, and resource allocation. In one project, I examined international debt statistics and GDP indicators from sources like the World Bank, performing regression analysis and correlation studies to explore relationships between debt levels, economic growth, and development metrics. The analysis revealed insights into how sustainable debt management correlates with improved education and health outcomes in developing countries, offering visualizations and forecasts to support informed economic policy decisions.

Health Sector

The health sector benefits immensely from data analysis in predicting outcomes, optimizing resources, and improving patient care. A key project focused on electronic health records (EHR) and public health datasets to predict patient readmission risks and disease patterns. Using machine learning models like regression and classification, the analysis identified factors such as demographics and comorbidities influencing heart disease or readmissions, enabling better preventive strategies, resource allocation in hospitals, and ultimately contributing to reduced healthcare costs and improved patient outcomes.

Consumer Behaviour

April 2024

GloBox, a beauty subscription service, recently launched a new project aimed at enhancing user experience and wanted to know if it can boost conversion rates. Conversion rates increased for group B compared to group A, supporting the expectation that the new project has a positive impact on conversions. However, no significant difference is observed in average spending between the two groups, suggesting that spending behavior may be influenced by external factors unrelated to the project. When disaggregated by gender and region, group B consistently demonstrates higher conversion rates than the control group, reinforcing the robustness of the conversion uplift across different user segments. A short-term increase in daily conversion rates is evident in the treatment group, indicating a potential novelty-driven response.
